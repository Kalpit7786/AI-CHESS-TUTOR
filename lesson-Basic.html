The Rules of Combat

Tap on any piece to learn its secrets, movements, and strategic value.

♙ The Pawn

Value: 1 Point
How it Moves:

Pawns are the foot soldiers. They move straight forward, one square at a time. However, on their very first move, they have the option to march forward two squares.

How it Captures:

Pawns are unique: they capture differently than they move! They capture enemy pieces by moving one square diagonally forward.

💡 Special Rule: Promotion! If a courageous pawn makes it all the way to the opposite side of the board, it magically transforms into any other piece (usually a Queen!).

♘ The Knight

Value: 3 Points
How it Moves & Captures:

The Knight moves in an "L" shape: two squares in one direction, and then one square at a 90-degree angle (or one square, then two squares).

💡 Special Power: Leaping! The Knight is the ONLY piece on the board that can jump over other pieces (both yours and the enemy's) to reach its destination.

♗ The Bishop

Value: 3 Points
How it Moves & Captures:

Bishops move diagonally across the board. They can move as many squares as they want in a single turn, as long as their path is not blocked.

Note: Because of how they move, a Bishop that starts on a white square will stay on white squares for the entire game! You have one Light-Squared Bishop and one Dark-Squared Bishop.

♖ The Rook

Value: 5 Points
How it Moves & Captures:

The Rook (sometimes called the Castle) is a heavy hitter. It moves in straight lines—forward, backward, left, or right—as many squares as it wants, as long as the path is clear.

💡 Pro Tip: Rooks are most powerful in the "endgame" when the board is clear, or when two Rooks link up on the same row!

♕ The Queen

Value: 9 Points
How it Moves & Captures:

The Queen is the most powerful piece on the board. She combines the powers of the Rook and the Bishop! She can move any number of squares in a straight line (horizontally or vertically) AND diagonally.

Because she is so valuable, you must protect her at all costs. Losing your Queen early usually means losing the game.

♔ The King

Value: The Entire Game
How it Moves & Captures:

The King is the most important piece, but also one of the weakest. He can only move one square at a time in any direction (forward, backward, left, right, or diagonally).

⚠️ The Golden Rule: You cannot make a move that puts your own King into danger (Check). If your King is attacked and cannot escape, it is Checkmate, and you lose!
